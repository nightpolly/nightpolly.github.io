This post is about: My experience at Eastern Europe’s first front-end conference.

SmartWeb, Bucharest

Oct. 24, 2013 2 minutes notes

Exactly a month ago I attended my very first front-end conference. This post is awfully late.

If you want to see a brilliantly deductive summary of the talks, read The SmartWeb Jellyfish Highjack by Radu Chelariu, who was one of the speakers.

SmartWeb was a big deal for me—not only because I wanted to see what a conference is like (TEDx events don’t count), but because we don’t have many of those in this corner of the continent. I really didn’t need convincing. Still, the list of speakers made a pretty good case.

I live in Bulgaria, but close to the Romanian border. So I decided I’ll make the two-hour drive to Bucharest early in the morning, leave the car somewhere for the day, and get back home in the evening.

In Bucharest, after losing a game of “who knows the city better” to the GPS, I found the venue. It was still early for the talks, so I spent some time watching the last preparations and checking Twitter. I saw a few of the speakers—some talking to attendees, some busy with their laptops, Carl Smith jumping in place…

Crowds are not really my thing, so even though the folks there seemed very nice, I felt super self-conscious the whole time. I’m one of those people who always sit in the back and listen, then sneak away unnoticed. Now I regret not talking to more of those guys and girls, but at the time I felt way out of my comfort zone.

My social awkwardness aside, the atmosphere was amazing. I remember thinking how all these people look like they know each other, like they belong there. Which made me realize that, until now, I’ve been doubting the existence of a web community in Eastern Europe. I knew there are some talented designers and developers here, but always assumed it’s just a handful of people… Very ignorant on my part. I should’ve been paying more attention.

A quick note about the sexism in the industry: I know it exists, and I was more that worried about that when I left for SmartWeb. I saw none of it there. Yes, most of the attendees and all of the speakers were men, and it so didn’t matter.

After the talks I went for a bite with a few friends who live in Bucharest. They saw me off to the after-party, which was a lot less crowded and I finally managed to relax a little. That and a few beers did it for my sleep-deprived brain, so unfortunately I couldn’t stay as long as I wanted. I did get some hugs before I left though.
